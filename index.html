CSCW 2026 Workshop

Co-Constructing Social Capital: Opportunities, Tensions, and Reimagining Community-Centered Design

Full-day workshop | 9:00 AM – 4:30 PM

This workshop brings together interdisciplinary researchers and practitioners to examine how community-centered design can evolve through community strength-based design and co-constructing with communities. It focuses on how sociotechnical systems, including AI-mediated systems, can better support belonging, trust, reciprocity, and resilient forms of social capital across diverse communities.

Call for Participation

We invite interdisciplinary researchers and practitioners working with subcultural, transnational, diaspora, and vulnerable communities, as well as those engaging with social capital, community-centered design, platform governance, AI-mediated interaction, and equity in digital environments.

We particularly welcome critical, reflexive, and strength-based approaches that build on existing community practices, capacities, and resilience. Our goal is to assemble a cohort that balances theoretical, methodological, and applied perspectives across diverse community contexts, geographic regions, and career stages.

Submission Format

  • 2–4 page position statement
  • ACM single-column format
  • Accessible PDF preferred

Submissions should include

  • A description of the author’s research or practice area
  • Their positionality and relationship to the communities they engage with
  • How their work connects to the workshop, especially in fostering or challenging social capital
  • A key tension, open question, or design opportunity in community-centered sociotechnical systems

Review Process

Submissions will be reviewed by the organizing team based on relevance, diversity of perspectives, and potential contribution to workshop discussions.
