Co-Constructing Social Capital: Opportunities, Tensions, and Reimagining Community-Centered Design

Saturday, October 10 OR 11, 2026
Agenda Call for Participation Contact Us

Overview

Communities are foundational social entities in which actors share needs, interests, and social practices, shaping the fabric of social experience. While sociotechnical systems have expanded the scale and capacity of community interaction, they also reshape participation in ways that can introduce distrust and harm, surface structural tensions, and create new dependencies—dynamics further intensified in the context of AI. Social capital offers a critical lens for understanding how communities form, sustain themselves, and evolve through networks, trust, and reciprocity.

This workshop foregrounds social capital as a critical lens for re-imagining community-centered design and calls for two paradigm shifts:

  • Community strength-based design: We reject deficit-oriented design logics that begin with what is broken or in need of fixing. Instead, we center what is strong within communities, examining how sociotechnical systems can recognize, reinforce, and extend existing practices, relationships, and forms of collective agency.
  • Co-constructing with communities: We move from designing for communities toward designing with communities as co-constitutive actors in sociotechnical systems, particularly in AI-mediated contexts.

This shift repositions communities from subjects of intervention to partners in shaping systems, and foregrounds the co-construction of meaningful and sustainable forms of social capital across diverse contexts, including creative and subcultural communities, transnational and diaspora networks, and communities experiencing structural vulnerability.

Call for Participation

All participants are required to attend the workshop in person.

We invite interdisciplinary researchers and practitioners working with subcultural, transnational, diaspora, and vulnerable communities, as well as those engaging with social capital, community-centered design, platform governance, AI-mediated interaction, and equity in digital environments.

We particularly welcome critical, reflexive, and strength-based approaches that build on existing community practices, capacities, and resilience. Our goal is to assemble a cohort that balances theoretical, methodological, and applied perspectives across diverse community contexts, geographic regions, and career stages.

Submission Format

  • 2–4 page position statement
  • ACM single-column format
  • Accessible PDF preferred

Submissions should include

  • A description of the author’s research or practice area
  • Their positionality and relationship to the communities they engage with
  • How their work connects to the workshop, especially in fostering or challenging social capital
  • A key tension, open question, or design opportunity in community-centered sociotechnical systems

Review Process

Submissions will be reviewed by the organizing team based on relevance, diversity of perspectives, and potential contribution to workshop discussions.
